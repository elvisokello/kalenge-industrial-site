TYPES OF TILAPIA

NILE TILAPIA

Nile tilapia (Oreochromis niloticus) is one of the most widely farmed and economically important fish species in aquaculture. It is known for its fast growth rate, adaptability to different environments, and ability to thrive in various farming systems

BLUE TILAPIA

Blue tilapia (Oreochromis aureus), also known as Israeli tilapia, is another important species in aquaculture. It shares many similarities with the Nile tilapia but has some unique characteristics that make it especially valuable in specific environments.

MOZAMBIQUE TILAPIA

Mozambique tilapia (Oreochromis mossambicus) is another significant species in aquaculture, closely related to the Nile and Blue tilapia. Originally from southern Africa, it has become widespread due to its adaptability and hardiness

RED TILAPIA

Red tilapia is a hybrid of various tilapia species, bred for its attractive color and desirable traits for aquaculture. It is not a specific species but rather a commercial variety known for its red to pinkish hue

TILAPIA ZILLII

Tilapia zillii, also known as Zille's tilapia or Redbelly tilapia, is a species of tilapia that is native to Northern and Western Africa and parts of the Middle East. It is widely recognized for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions, making it a significant species for aquaculture and environmental management.

WAMI TILAPIA

Wami Tilapia (Oreochromis urolepis hornorum) is a lesser-known but notable species of tilapia that is often used in aquaculture.
